# Puzzle-to-Competency Matrix

Every puzzle in the prototype maps to at least one learning objective. No puzzle exists without a mapping. This table is the check: if anything ever appears in the prototype that is not in this table, cut it or justify it.

## Matrix

| Beat | Puzzle | Primary LO | Secondary LOs | Mechanic | Success criterion |
|------|--------|-----------|---------------|----------|--------------------|
| 1 | First-action choice after the opening frame | LO1 Role boundaries | LO2 (Tylenol implicit) | Multiple choice, one correct | Learner selects "sit Mia down, gather facts, take it from here" and reads the feedback that distinguishes observation from clinical assessment |
| 2 | Match each Five Right to its failure for the Tylenol request | LO2 Five Rights | LO1 (reinforces scope), LO3 (what "right task" means in Oregon) | Match / drag-drop | Learner correctly matches all five Rights to their failure reason; reads the refusal script |
| 3 | Sort actions into In scope / Out of scope / Escalate | LO3 Oregon scope | LO1 (role boundaries), LO4 (escalation option is a third bucket) | Sort into three buckets | Learner sorts all ten items correctly, including the emergency epi / glucagon carve-out item |
| 4a | Sequence the escalation calls after the change-of-condition cue | LO4 Recognition and escalation | LO1 (RN is the decision-maker) | Order / sequence | Learner orders RN → parent → 911 correctly and understands why |
| 4b | Pick correct scripts for RN, parent, (and 911 if applicable) | LO4 Recognition and escalation | LO5 (content overlaps with documentation) | Multi-select from pool | Learner picks the factual, objective, specific script for each call and rejects the vague or diagnostic one |
| 5 | Fill the incident form | LO5 Documentation | LO4 (escalations recorded), LO1 (only observations, not diagnoses) | Drag / select into form fields | Learner fills every field with the objective, timestamped, named entry and rejects the interpretive or vague one |

## Puzzle validity check — each puzzle is NOT trivia

- **Beat 1** is not trivia: it tests a judgment (what to do first under ambiguous pressure). A learner who memorizes "the answer" without understanding the scope logic will miss the same type of question phrased differently.
- **Beat 2** is not trivia: it tests whether the learner can actually run a real request through the Five Rights framework. A learner can't pass by memorizing the Five Rights terms — they must apply them.
- **Beat 3** is not trivia: several items are close-call (ibuprofen from the backpack, clearing to return to class) and require scope reasoning, not recall.
- **Beat 4a** tests sequence reasoning under a time constraint implicit in the narrative, not call-number memorization.
- **Beat 4b** is not trivia: it tests the learner's ability to distinguish objective vs. interpretive language — a communication skill that transfers to every escalation a UAP will ever make.
- **Beat 5** is not trivia: it tests documentation quality, which has operational consequences for the RN who reviews the record.

## Coverage check — every LO is exercised

- LO1 — exercised in Beats 1, 3, 5.
- LO2 — exercised in Beat 2.
- LO3 — exercised in Beat 3.
- LO4 — exercised in Beats 4a and 4b.
- LO5 — exercised in Beat 5.

All five LOs are covered. LO2 is the most concentrated (one beat); the others are reinforced across beats, which the literature on retention supports.

## Ground rule

From the brief: "If it doesn't map to a learning objective, cut it." This matrix is the one source of truth for that rule. Any puzzle added during build — a transition moment, a supporting interaction, a visual flourish — either shows up here with an LO or comes out of the build.
